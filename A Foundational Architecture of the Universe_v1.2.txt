## 1. Abstract

This paper proposes that the universe can be described as a structured information-processing system consisting of a lattice-based substrate operating under global rotational constraints.

The observable universe is defined as a dynamically evolving configuration of states instantiated over this lattice substrate. Crucially, this configuration does not remain static in position, but follows a continuous, structured trajectory within the rotating system.

In this framework, the universe behaves as an expanding, coherently evolving state-domain that progresses along a path within the lattice structure. This progression gives rise to effective cyclic or rolling dynamics, through which cosmological expansion history can be interpreted.

## 2. Core Hypotheses (Priority Set)

### 2.1 Structural Postulate: Overlapping Lattice Substrate

The fundamental substrate of the universe is defined as a discretely addressable volumetric lattice composed of overlapping units.

A 3 × 3 × 3 (27-unit) configuration is proposed as a candidate minimal stable structure under overlapping constraints, though the framework is not restricted to this specific configuration.

This lattice constitutes the underlying structural medium capable of supporting:

* Information persistence
* State propagation
* Layer-based accumulation

---

### 2.2 Representational Postulate: Universe as Displayed State

The observable universe is not identified with the lattice substrate itself, but with a configuration of states defined over it.

The lattice functions as a structural display medium, while physical reality corresponds to the evolving configuration rendered upon this medium.

This displayed configuration forms a coherent domain ("universe domain") that evolves continuously over the substrate.

---

### 2.3 Dynamical Postulate: Global Rotation and Rolling Trajectory

The lattice substrate operates under a global rotational constraint defined at the level of the entire structure.

Within this rotating structure, the universe domain does not merely expand in place. Instead, it progresses along a continuous trajectory across the lattice, maintaining coherence while evolving.

This trajectory can be interpreted as a rolling-like evolution of the universe domain over the substrate, in which:

* Expansion corresponds to growth of the domain
* Translation corresponds to progression along the lattice
* Rotation induces position-dependent effective dynamics

The combined effect produces a rolling or cyclic progression of the universe domain within the lattice structure.

---

### 2.4 Temporal Postulate: Commitment Layer Accumulation

Time is defined as the ordered accumulation of irreversible state commitments across the lattice.

Each global update of lattice states constitutes a layer, forming a directional sequence.

The rolling progression of the universe domain is realized through successive commitment layers.

---

### 2.5 Gravitational Postulate: Information Density Gradient

Gravitational phenomena arise from non-uniform distributions of committed information across the lattice.

Regions of higher commitment density produce structural gradients affecting state propagation and the trajectory of the universe domain.

This results in:

* Effective curvature-like behavior
* Variation in local evolution rates

---

## 3. Structural Implications

The combined postulates imply:

1. The observable universe behaves as an expanding domain that progresses along a structured path within a rotating lattice substrate.

2. Apparent motion and large-scale dynamics correspond to coherent domain evolution rather than independent motion within an external space.

3. Cosmological expansion, deceleration, and re-acceleration phases can be interpreted as position-dependent dynamical regimes encountered along the rolling trajectory.
