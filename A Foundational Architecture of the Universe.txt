## 1. Abstract

This paper proposes that the universe can be described as a structured information-processing substrate with a specific geometric and dynamical architecture. Rather than introducing additional unknown fields or constants, the model defines a discrete yet overlapping lattice system operating under global rotational dynamics.

The framework introduces a 27-unit overlapping lattice as a candidate substrate structure and interprets cosmological expansion, temporal directionality, and gravitational effects as emergent consequences of system-level information organization and constraint propagation.

This document establishes the core structural hypotheses required for priority claim. No claim is made regarding empirical validation at this stage.

## 2. Core Hypotheses (Priority Set)

### 2.1 Structural Postulate: 27-Unit Overlapping Lattice

The fundamental substrate of the universe is defined as a discrete volumetric unit composed of 27 nodes arranged in a 3 × 3 × 3 configuration.

These units are not isolated. Each unit partially overlaps with adjacent units such that:

* Neighboring lattice units share boundary nodes
* The system forms a continuous but discretely addressable structure

This structure is defined as the minimal stable configuration capable of supporting:

* Information persistence
* Inter-unit propagation
* Layer-based accumulation (as defined in the Commitment framework)

Within this model, the previously defined “substrate” is identified with this overlapping lattice system.

---

### 2.2 Dynamical Postulate: Global Rotational Constraint

The lattice substrate is not static. The system operates under a global rotational constraint defined at the level of the entire structure.

This rotation is not introduced as an external force but as a boundary condition of the system.

Consequences of this postulate:

* Local regions experience differential effective acceleration depending on position within the global structure
* Observed cosmological expansion is interpreted as a positional effect within this rotational system

No absolute external reference frame is assumed. All observable dynamics emerge from internal relational structure.

---

### 2.3 Temporal Postulate: Layer Accumulation as Time

Consistent with the Commitment Layer framework, time is defined as the sequential accumulation of irreversible information states.

Within the lattice model:

* Each stable state transition across the lattice constitutes a commitment event
* A complete system-wide update corresponds to one layer

Therefore:

* Time is not an independent dimension
* Time is the ordered accumulation of lattice-state commitments

This postulate directly maps to the previously defined commitment layer structure and does not introduce a new temporal mechanism.

---

### 2.4 Gravitational Postulate: Information Density Gradient

Gravitational phenomena are interpreted as emergent effects of non-uniform information density within the lattice.

Specifically:

* Regions with higher commitment density create structural gradients in the lattice
* These gradients affect propagation paths and state transition costs

This results in:

* Effective curvature-like behavior
* Time dilation effects via variation in layer accumulation rate

This definition is consistent with the prior formulation in which gravity is a consequence of commitment density, now grounded in a specific substrate structure.

## 3. Structural Implications (Non-Exhaustive)

The combination of the above postulates implies the following:

1. Cosmological expansion is not driven by external energy injection but arises from the interaction between global rotational constraint and lattice occupancy.

2. Large-scale structure may exhibit weak anisotropies reflecting underlying lattice organization.

3. Temporal directionality is enforced by irreversible state commitment and cannot be inverted within the system.

These implications are presented as structural consequences rather than quantitative predictions.
